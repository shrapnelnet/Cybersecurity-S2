Malware

  • Malware

    What is Malware

    Malware is piece of code created with the intention of causing negative effects for the user. This can because harmful results such as:

    • devices becoming unusable
    • compromised data
    • stolen credentials
    • using or buying products/services on your behalf
    • unauthorized access to content of the infected device

    Types of Malware:

  • Types

    Viruses

    A virus is a type of software created to infect legitimate software with itself and using that to replicate itself across various network and onto other files if it is allowed to activate. due to the ability to easily spread an unchecked virus can massively harm any device it gets onto as it could risk replicating onto other files on the device making it even harder to remove.

  • Types

    Trojans

    Trojans are another type of malware.They function by disguising as legitimate software.If the software is capable of getting onto the computer there can be various risks such as:

    • Captured data
    • Unauthorized access
    • Stolen data

    Trojans also tend to cause infected devices to gain spyware which is used to monitor a users activities without their knowledge or consent.

    Trojans are typically used as a backdoor for various cybercriminals for various reasons such as gaining control of a system or installing even more malware onto the system

  • Types

    Worms

    A worm is a form of malware very much similar to a virus in the form of replicating across networks but shares some differences. Some of these differences being:

    • A worm is a standalone malware program and is capable of replicating without the need for the user to open anything
    • A worm requires a host to attach itself to in oder to replicate
    • Worms are not used for the same purpose as Viruses either

    Worms are typically used to slow down systems and cause disruptions. They are able to do this by consuming bandwidth of the system and infecting a large number of devices

  • Types

    ransomware

    ransomware is a type of malware used from hackers to attempting to receive payment from you.This can be done if your computer is infected with ransomware.

    Ransomware is used to withhold access to your computer and the data within it. In some cases ransomware can even spread onto other devices onto the network.

    Once the ransomware is on a system attackers threaten users with their data and ask for compensation with the idea of returning the data if they do. However, it is considered a bad idea to pay this for various reasons such as:

    • No confirmation that access will be granted back to your device and data
    • The computer will still be infected after
    • There is higher risk of further attacks as you could be seen as vulnerable to hackers
    • You are giving money to criminals

  • Types

    Adware

    Adware is a lot more harmless comparing to the other malware but still negatively affects the user experience. This is done by forcing advertisements onto the users computer for the purpose of generating revenue.This can be done by:

    • pop-ups
    • banners
    • direct injections on used webpages

    This adware typically comes with free software from untrusted sources.This adware can potentially slow your device by consuming a networks' bandwidth. Despite not being inherently malicious, adware is overall just intrusive and bothersome and will harm user experience. In some cases the adware can even direct users to harmful websites or download unwanted software onto the users device

Mitigation

  • Mitigation

    What is mitigation

    Mitigation in the context of cyber-security refers to the strategies and measures taken to reduce the risk of cyber threats and attacks. This includes practices such as keeping systems and software up-to-date, using antivirus software, avoiding unknown links and downloads, employing firewalls, and using Virtual Private Networks (VPNs). The goal of mitigation is to protect systems and data from unauthorized access and potential damage, ensuring the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of data.

  • Mitigation Methods

    Keep your system up to date.

    Keeping your OS and drivers up to date is crucial for mitigating the chances of your system being contaminated with malware as this update usually include patches for security vulnerabilities that have been found. By not updating , you leave your system at risk of being exposed to these vulnerabilities , which can lead to malware can use to gain unauthorized access.Updates also often include improved security features that improve your systems ability to detect and prevent malware attacks.

  • Mitigation Methods

    Anti-Virus Software

    Using Anti-virus software is great method to mitigate of the risks of getting malware because it is designed to detect, prevent and remove malicious files. Antivirus software works by using a table of known malware signatures to check files for matches to identify potential threats.

  • Mitigation Methods

    Avoid Unknown links and downloads.

    Avoiding downloading from unknown links and sources a crucial step to mitigate the risks of getting malware.Untrusted sources often have files that contain malicious files, which can infect your computer once downloaded and installed.By only downloading from official sources and not opening links you dont know , you significantly reduce the risk of your system being infected with malware.

  • Mitigation Methods

    Firewall Usage

    Using a firewall is another great way to stop yourself from being infected with malware. Firewalls serve as a barrier between your system and the internet, blocking unauthorized access while still allowing you to communicate with the web. This means they can prevent malware from accessing your system as they can block potentially harmful incoming traffic and specific applications fom sending information which stop the malware from being able to spread.

  • Mitigation Methods

    VPNs

    The Use of a VPN is crucial step in mitigating the chances of getting malware. A VPN encrypts your internet connection making it harder for hackers to get access to and change your data , which is a method to spread malware. A VPN also maks your IP address which helps protect from targeted malware attacks overall improving your internet security.
